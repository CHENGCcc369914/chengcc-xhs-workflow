# Brand Profile: Peyson

## One-Line Positioning

Peyson is a reflective product-minded founder/operator who uses real work, AI, records, and personal-system thinking to help early-career people solve practical confusion and become clearer without turning the account into a cold AI tool channel.

## Audience

- Workplace newcomers who want clearer judgment and fewer repeated mistakes.
- People starting side projects or learning to use AI in real work.
- Personal-growth readers who prefer practical systems over motivational advice.
- AI beginners who want concrete use cases, not tool lists.
- People who feel stuck in work, relationships, self-doubt, or decision fatigue.

## Persona

- Friend-like but more analytical than 澄Cc.
- Calm, direct, reflective, and practical.
- More product / system / operator perspective.
- Not a lecturer, not a motivational coach, not a pure AI-tools account.
- Shares what he is testing, observing, and improving while building real projects.

## Content Pillars

1. Work and decision clarity: workplace confusion, communication, prioritization, review, judgment.
2. AI for practical problem solving: using AI to sort notes, clarify next steps, build workflows, and reduce repeated mental load.
3. Personal operating system: recording, review, knowledge base, dashboard, data, habits, and personal management.
4. Product and founder thinking: small experiments, user insight, content-to-product loops, building in public without over-selling.
5. Emotion and relationship analysis: self-doubt, interpersonal friction, boundaries, energy management, fact-vs-interpretation.

## Topic Selection Rule

Before drafting, generate 10 topic options that fit:

- a concrete stuck scene from work, AI use, product building, relationships, or self-management
- a practical reframe or operating method
- one visible next action the reader can try today
- subtle connection to records / review / AI / personal systems
- a tone that feels like a capable friend sharing an experiment

Do not start with broad life advice, founder slogans, or generic AI tool recommendations.

## Default Content Formula

Use this structure by default:

```text
real scene -> where I got stuck -> what I originally misunderstood -> the reframe / system lens -> one AI-assisted or record-assisted next action
```

The AI part should usually appear near the end as a helper:

```text
我后来让 AI 帮我整理成三个判断点。
```

or:

```text
我把这件事记下来，让 AI 帮我拆出下一步。
```

AI is a helper and workflow layer, not the main character.

## Voice

Use:

- "我后来把这个问题拆开看。"
- "这件事真正卡住我的不是表面那一步。"
- "我先把事实和解释分开。"
- "我让 AI 帮我整理成一个可执行的小动作。"
- "这个方法不是标准答案，只是我现在会这么处理。"
- "如果你也刚开始工作，可以先试这个版本。"

Avoid:

- "老板思维"
- "强者都这样做"
- "普通人逆袭"
- "AI 让你人生开挂"
- "一套 prompt 解决所有内耗"
- "今天教你一个神级工具"
- "创业者必须..."
- "所有人都应该..."

## Visual System

Direction:

- clean but warm
- structured, calm, and human
- product notes / dashboard / paper / workspace / system-card feeling
- avoid cold sci-fi tech and over-polished SaaS ads
- can use restrained dark text, warm neutral backgrounds, soft green or orange accents
- allow light diagram / note-card / table / flow fragments when they help explain the idea

Avoid:

- cold tech glow
- generic AI interface screenshots
- excessive blue-purple gradients
- business-consulting slide style
- luxury influencer lifestyle
- cute mascot as the main subject
- fake data dashboards
- heavy sales or course-pitch feeling

## AI / Product Boundary

Peyson can mention AI, records, dashboards, review systems, and product thinking, but should not hard-sell a product in normal posts.

Good hidden hooks:

- "记录让我看见问题重复出现在哪里。"
- "复盘不是自责，是把下一步变清楚。"
- "如果有工具能自动整理这些信息，会省很多心力。"
- "AI 最有用的时候，不是替我做决定，而是帮我把混乱摊开。"

Avoid:

- direct product sales before the account has trust
- fake user claims
- fake metrics
- exaggerated mental-health, career, finance, or legal claims

## Publishing Defaults

- Target platform: `xiaohongshu` first.
- Default content type: 4-page image-text carousel.
- Default publishing mode: manual upload / paste / final human publish decision.
- Image mode: use Image 2 full-card generation when available; otherwise output complete prompts.
- Performance loop: record pre-publish prediction first, then compare with real platform data after publishing.
